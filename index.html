Calendar Color Guide
  
    
    
      🔴 Red — Increasing (Too High)       God dial trending upward. Jet speed rising above target or geopotential height dropping — amplifying trough, bad for Toronto warmth.     
  
  
    
    
      🔵 Blue — Decreasing (Too Low)       God dial trending downward. Jet collapsing, geopotential recovering slowly — stalling pattern, low pressure orphaned over Toronto.     
  
  
    
    
      🟣 Purple — In Target Range       All dials near optimal. Jet 130–160 km/h, geopotential ≥ 9350m, surface pressure ≥ 1015 hPa. God would leave it here.     
  
  
Data Sources & Metrics Used (Open-Meteo ERA5 + Forecast API — Free, No Key)
  
    
      200 hPa Wind Speed (km/h)       True jet core level in May/summer. Target: 130–200 km/h. Below 130 = sub-jet, too low. Above 200 = too aggressive.     
    
      300 hPa Wind Speed (km/h)       Jet steering level. Target: 130–160 km/h. Official NOAA threshold: 130 km/h. Used to confirm jet presence.     
    
      300 hPa Geopotential Height (m)       Trough/ridge indicator. Normal May Toronto: ~9350–9450m. Below 9200m = deep cold trough. Above 9450m = ridge building.     
    
      500 hPa Wind Speed (km/h)       Mid-level steering. Shows wave amplitude indirectly. Target: 80–130 km/h over Toronto for progressive flow.     
    
      Sea Level Pressure (hPa)       Surface confirmation. Target: ≥1015 hPa over Toronto. Below 1008 hPa = low pressure dominating = dial too high upstream.     
    
      God Dial Score (composite)       Weighted composite of all 5 metrics vs targets. Score 0–100. Purple: 55–75. Red: trending toward extremes. Blue: collapsed.
